The Incentive Problem With Flat-Fee Cloud Tools

Before we talk about what Hero Savings does, let's talk about what most cloud optimization tools do — and why it doesn't work as well as it should.

A typical cloud management platform charges you somewhere between $500 and $5,000 per month, regardless of outcome. Their incentive is to keep you subscribed, not necessarily to maximize your savings. In fact, a cynical reading of the flat-fee model suggests that if they save you too much too fast, you might downgrade your plan or cancel entirely.

The Flat-Fee Trap: When a tool charges the same fee whether it saves you $500 or $50,000, its incentive is to look busy — not to actually reduce your bill. Dashboards and alerts keep you subscribed. Real savings might get you to cancel.

This isn't a conspiracy theory. It's basic economics. Misaligned incentives produce misaligned behavior. Consultancies figured this out decades ago — the best ones charge a percentage of the value they deliver. Investment bankers charge a success fee. Recruiting firms charge a placement fee. Performance-based compensation is one of the oldest and most effective ways to align two parties.

Cloud cost optimization was overdue for the same model.

How Hero Savings Works: The 15% Performance Fee Model

Hero Savings is Cloud Hero AI's flagship product at cloudheroai.com/savings/. The model is simple: connect your cloud account, let the AI scan for waste, approve the fixes you want applied, and then pay 15% of your verified monthly savings — and nothing else.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

Step 1: Connect Your Cloud Account

Hero Savings integrates with AWS, Google Cloud (GCP), and Microsoft Azure via read-only access initially. You grant permission through standard IAM roles — no credentials stored, no broad administrative access required. The connection takes under five minutes.

Step 2: AI-Powered Waste Scan

Once connected, Cloud Hero AI's engine analyzes your entire infrastructure footprint. It looks across dozens of waste categories:

  • Idle and underutilized compute — instances running at <5% CPU for 30+ days
  • Orphaned storage — unattached EBS volumes, stale GCS buckets, Azure disks with no owner
  • Over-provisioned databases — RDS or Cloud SQL instances sized 4× bigger than their peak load
  • Reserved Instance coverage gaps — on-demand spend that should be covered by commitments
  • Wasteful data transfer patterns — cross-region traffic that could be rerouted
  • Unused load balancers, NAT gateways, and Elastic IPs
  • Oversized Kubernetes node pools with headroom you're not using

Step 3: Review and Approve Fixes

You get a full itemized report — every finding, its estimated monthly cost, and the recommended action. You decide what to implement. Some fixes are one-click automated. Others require a human decision (like terminating an instance you thought was idle but turns out is used once a quarter). Nothing is applied without your approval.

You're always in control. Hero Savings never modifies your infrastructure automatically. Every fix goes through an approval flow. You can implement changes yourself or let Cloud Hero AI's automation handle it — your choice.

Step 4: Verify the Savings

After fixes are applied, Cloud Hero AI monitors your billing data for 30 days to verify the actual savings. Not projected savings — actual reduction in your cloud bill, compared against a baseline. This is the number the fee is calculated on.

Step 5: Pay 15% of Verified Savings

At the end of each month, you receive an invoice for 15% of your verified savings. If we found nothing actionable, you pay nothing. If we reduced your bill by $10,000, you owe $1,500 — and you're still $8,500 ahead.

Why $12 Billion in Cloud Waste Persists Despite All the Tools

If the tools exist, why does Gartner still estimate $12 billion in annual cloud waste globally? A few reasons:

1. Tool Sprawl Without Action

Many organizations have three or four cloud cost tools and none of them talk to each other. They generate recommendations that sit unactioned in dashboards. According to the Flexera 2026 report, 74% of organizations say they have cost optimization tools but lack the time to act on recommendations.

2. Misaligned Ownership

Cloud costs sit at the intersection of finance, engineering, and operations. Nobody owns it completely. Engineers don't want to touch production. Finance doesn't understand the infrastructure. The result is paralysis.

3. Fear of Breaking Things

Downsizing a database instance or terminating what looks like an idle server carries real risk. Without confidence that a resource is truly unused, the default is inaction. This is why the Hero Savings approval workflow matters — it gives teams the data confidence to act.

Warning: The most expensive cloud waste isn't what you can see — it's the accumulation of small, unreviewed costs across dozens of services that no one questions because they're below the threshold for individual attention.

4. Flat-Fee Tools Remove the Urgency

When a vendor charges you a flat fee whether you act or not, there's no urgency on their side. Hero Savings is different: if you don't save, neither do we. That creates a fundamentally different relationship — one where Cloud Hero AI is actively working to find more waste, not just reporting on it.
